🕐Daily Ops Flow
Start of day → end of day
01

PRE-SHIFT: BEFORE YOU LEAVE THE YARD

6:30–7:00 AM

Truck Check

Walk around — tires, lights, doors, hitch. Check fuel. Any damage gets logged before you roll. No exceptions.


7:00 AM — Equipment Load-Out

Dollies, straps, floor runners, brooms, signage, PPE. If it's not on the truck, you're going back for it.


7:15 AM — Route Review + Huddle

Team Leader reviews all jobs for the day. Flag any known challenges — crawl spaces, estate, demolitions. Address hazards before wheels roll.


7:30 AM — First Job Depart

On time. Every time. Customer is already expecting you.

02

MID-SHIFT: BETWEEN JOBS

  • Truck cleared, swept, and ready for next load
  • Job closed in system — revenue captured before departing
  • Team Leader checks in with dispatch on next job ETA
  • Crew hydrated, fed, not running on empty
  • Any customer issues flagged to TL immediately — never buried
  • Review next job notes: access, special items, customer flags
03

END OF SHIFT

EOD

Truck Return & Inspection

Full unload, truck swept clean, any damage documented with photos before parking.


Job Reconciliation

All jobs closed in system. Revenue reconciled. No open loops.


Team Leader Debrief

Any wins, misses, near-misses, complaints, or customer shoutouts go in the debrief. What happened today shapes tomorrow.

🔑 Daily Non-Negotiables

You are the brand the moment you leave the yard. Uniform on, attitude locked in, truck clean. There's no "off" switch when you're in the vehicle.

⚡Job Execution
How we run every single job
01

On Arrival

  • Pull up clean, park professionally — you're being watched from the moment you arrive
  • Uniforms on, truck presentable, team sharp
  • Introduce yourself by name, confirm the job scope before touching anything
  • Do a full walkthrough with the customer — confirm what's going, what's staying
02

During the Job

  • Protect floors, walls, and doorframes — use runners and pads
  • Work efficiently but never rushed — mistakes happen when you rush
  • Communicate constantly with your partner — call out weight, obstacles, tight spaces
  • Keep the customer updated if scope changes mid-job
  • Never leave a customer alone in a awkward situation — always have a TL nearby
03

Job Close

  • Final walkthrough with customer — confirm everything was taken, nothing left behind
  • Collect payment before leaving — no exceptions
  • Ask for the review: "If we did a great job today, we'd really appreciate a Google review"
  • Close job in system immediately — revenue captured on site
  • Sweep the area, leave it cleaner than you found it
💰Sales & Upsell
Every job is a sales opportunity
01

The Quote

Always quote confidently. Show the price on the truck, walk them through it. Never apologize for the price — own it. Our service is worth it.

02

Add-Ons & Upsells

  • Look for additional items during the walkthrough — "What about that shed out back?"
  • Offer labor-only if they want to keep items but need moving help
  • Mention seasonal cleanouts, storage units, garages
  • If they hesitate on price — remind them of the value: same-day, all-inclusive, no dump fees
03

Referrals

At job close: "Do you know anyone else who could use our services? We take great care of referrals." Every referral is a warm lead — treat it like gold.

💡 Sales Mindset

You're not selling junk removal. You're selling relief, space, and peace of mind. Lead with that energy and the close takes care of itself.

⭐CX Standards
How we treat every customer, every time
01

The Greeting

High energy, warm, professional. First impressions are made in seconds. Smile, make eye contact, use their name if you know it.

02

Communication

  • Confirm arrival window the morning of — never let a customer wonder where you are
  • If you're running late, call before the window closes — not after
  • Speak clearly, no jargon, no inside jokes around customers
  • If a customer has a complaint — listen fully before responding, never get defensive
03

The Review Ask

"We really appreciate your business today. If you felt we did a great job, we'd love a Google review — it takes 30 seconds and means the world to our team."


Ask every. single. job. No exceptions.
